Result & Conclusion

Result

The dose–response curve of acetylcholine (ACh) on isolated guinea pig ileum smooth muscle was found to be a sigmoid-shaped curve when plotted against log concentration of the drug.

Increasing concentrations of acetylcholine produced graded contractions of the ileum due to stimulation of muscarinic receptors present on the smooth muscle.

In the presence of atropine, the dose-response curve shifted to the right side without reduction of the maximum response, indicating competitive antagonism.

Conclusion

Acetylcholine acts as an agonist producing contraction via muscarinic receptors on guinea pig ileum smooth muscle.

Atropine acts as a competitive antagonist, causing a parallel rightward shift of the dose-response curve without reducing the maximum response.

This demonstrates receptor-specific blockade and is widely used in pharmacology to characterize muscarinic antagonists.

Note: Many institutions now use virtual simulations (e.g., Guinea Pig Ileum software) to replace animal experiments in pharmacology laboratories.
