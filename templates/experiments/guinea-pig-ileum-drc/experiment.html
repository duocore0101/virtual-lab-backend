Experimental Procedure

  1. Sacrifice guinea pig by cervical dislocation or CO₂ asphyxiation.
  2. Open abdomen and locate ileum; cut 2–3 cm segment avoiding Peyer's patches.
  3. Flush lumen with Tyrode's solution and mount tissue in organ bath at 37°C aerated with 95% O₂ + 5% CO₂.
  4. Apply resting tension 0.5–1 g and equilibrate for 30–45 minutes washing every 10 minutes.
  5. Record normal DRC for agonist:
    • Add increasing doses of acetylcholine (10⁻⁸ to 10⁻⁴ M).
    • Allow contact time 30–60 seconds.
    • Wash tissue 3–4 times.
    • Allow 5 minute cycle between doses.
  6. Effect of antagonist:
    • Add atropine (10⁻⁸ M) and incubate 10–15 minutes.
    • Record new DRC in presence of atropine.
    • Optional higher atropine concentrations for dose ratio calculation.
  7. Plot % maximum response vs log dose.
