Theory/Principle

Principle

Serotonin (5-HT) produces a dose-dependent contraction of the longitudinal smooth muscle of the rat fundus.

The responses follow a sigmoidal log dose-response curve, where a linear portion allows accurate comparison of drug potency.

In the Three-Point Bioassay Method:

  • Two standard doses (S1 and S2) are selected such that:
    S1 produces ~20–40% response
    S2 produces ~60–80% response
  • One test dose (T) is selected whose response lies between S1 and S2.
  • Responses are recorded in a randomized or Latin square sequence (S1–S2–T, S2–T–S1, T–S1–S2) over multiple cycles.
  • Average responses are used to calculate potency using interpolation or formula.
