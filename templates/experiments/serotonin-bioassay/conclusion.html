Result & Conclusion

Result

The concentration of the unknown 5-HT (serotonin) solution in the given test sample was found to be:

9.2 μg/mL

(Approximately 9.24 μg/mL using precise interpolation calculation.)

Potency comparison:

  • The unknown sample is slightly less potent than the standard stock (10 μg/mL).
  • A larger volume (0.4 mL) of test solution was required to match the response of ~0.355 mL of standard solution.

Conclusion

The experiment confirms the validity of the Three-Point Bioassay Method for determining the potency of serotonin (5-HT) using rat fundus strip.

The assay demonstrated:

  • Successful bracketing of the test response (42 mm lies between 23 mm and 57 mm).
  • Good reproducibility (cycle variations less than 5 mm).
  • Stable tissue preparation with minimal tachyphylaxis.

Therefore, the bioassay is reliable and suitable for estimation of unknown 5-HT concentration in student practical settings.
