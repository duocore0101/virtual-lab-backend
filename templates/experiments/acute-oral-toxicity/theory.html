Theory/Principle

Pre-test Preparation (24 h Before)

  • Animals are acclimatised to laboratory conditions.
  • Examine animals for pre-existing abnormalities or illness.
  • Only healthy, fasted animals are selected for dosing.
  • Unsuitable animals are excluded from the study.

Dose Selection & Administration

  • OECD 423 follows a stepwise dose progression method.
  • Common fixed doses: 5, 50, 300, 2000 mg/kg.
  • Test substance administered orally using gavage needle.
  • Animals are fasted overnight prior to dosing.

Sequential Testing Procedure

  • Initial test: 3 female rats at selected dose.
  • If mortality observed → proceed to lower dose.
  • If no mortality → proceed to higher dose.
  • Stepwise testing reduces animal usage.
  • LD₅₀ range is estimated based on mortality pattern.

Observation Period (14 Days)

  • Observe animals continuously for first 4 hours.
  • Daily monitoring for 14 days.
  • Record clinical signs: tremors, convulsions, salivation, lethargy.
  • Monitor body weight and food intake.
  • Record mortality, if any.

Data Analysis & Classification

  • Calculate percentage mortality at each dose.
  • Estimate approximate LD₅₀ value.
  • Classify substance according to GHS toxicity categories.
  • Category 1–5 based on LD₅₀ range.
