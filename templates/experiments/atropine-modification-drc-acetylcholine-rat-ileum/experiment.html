Experimental Procedure

  1. Mount isolated rat ileum in an organ bath containing aerated physiological solution and allow stabilization for 30–60 minutes.
  2. Add a small dose of acetylcholine to check tissue sensitivity.
  3. Add increasing concentrations or volumes of acetylcholine and record the contraction responses.
  4. After each dose, wash the tissue and allow full relaxation before adding the next dose.
  5. Plot the normal dose-response curve of acetylcholine.
  6. Add atropine to the organ bath (e.g., 0.1–1 μg/ml) and allow 5–10 minutes equilibration.
  7. Repeat acetylcholine doses in the presence of atropine and record the responses.
  8. Higher concentrations of atropine may be used to observe dose-dependent rightward shift.
  9. Wash the tissue to confirm reversibility of antagonism.
