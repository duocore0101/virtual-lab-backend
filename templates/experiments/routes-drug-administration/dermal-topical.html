Dermal Topical – Preparation & Method

  • Shave or clip hair 24 hours before application (avoid abrasions).
  • Clean area with mild soap/water and dry thoroughly.
  • Apply cream/ointment/gel evenly (0.1–0.5 mL/cm²).
  • For occlusive method, cover with gauze/plastic wrap to enhance penetration.
  • Monitor for irritation, erythema, or grooming behavior.

Note: Occlusive dressing may increase absorption 10–100× but also increases irritation risk.

Dermal Topical Administration

Overview

The topical route involves applying drugs directly to body surfaces such as skin or mucous membranes. It is widely used for local dermatological testing, irritation studies, and transdermal delivery.

Advantages & Disadvantages

  • Advantages: Non-invasive, avoids first-pass metabolism.
  • Useful for local skin and transdermal studies.
  • Disadvantages: Poor systemic absorption for many drugs.
  • Risk of grooming/licking and local irritation.
  • Variable absorption due to skin thickness and site.

Pharmacokinetics

  • Local Effect: Rapid action at application site.
  • Systemic (Transdermal): Slow, sustained absorption.
  • Absorption via stratum corneum barrier.
  • Bioavailability often low unless occlusive method used.
  • Common Sites: Dorsal back/flanks (rodents), abdomen/back (rabbits).
