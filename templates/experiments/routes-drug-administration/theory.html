Theory

Routes of Drug Administration in Experimental Animals

Drug administration in experimental animals is a fundamental component of preclinical pharmacological and toxicological studies. The choice of route depends on the objective of the study and the experimental design.

Factors Influencing Route Selection

The selected route is determined by the physicochemical properties of the drug, desired onset and duration of action, required bioavailability, and ethical considerations related to animal welfare.

Pharmacokinetic Considerations (ADME)

Different routes significantly influence absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion (ADME) of drugs. These factors ultimately affect systemic exposure and pharmacokinetic behavior.

Impact on Bioavailability

The route of administration directly determines the bioavailability of a drug. For example, oral administration may undergo first-pass metabolism, whereas intravenous administration provides complete systemic availability.

Scientific & Ethical Importance

Proper selection and execution of drug administration routes ensure accurate experimental outcomes while maintaining compliance with ethical guidelines and minimizing stress or harm to experimental animals.
