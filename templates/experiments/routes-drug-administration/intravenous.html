Intravenous (IV) – Technique

  • Warm tail (37–40°C) to dilate veins.
  • Restrain animal in tube; extend tail.
  • Swab injection site with alcohol.
  • Insert needle bevel-up at 10–30° angle into lateral tail vein.
  • Aspirate gently (blood flash confirms placement).
  • Inject slowly (10–30 seconds for bolus).
  • Withdraw needle and apply gentle pressure.

Important: Avoid extravasation; improper injection may cause tissue necrosis or thrombosis.

Intravenous (IV) Route

Overview

The intravenous route delivers drugs directly into the bloodstream, providing 100% bioavailability and immediate onset of action. It is essential for pharmacokinetic studies and acute interventions.

Advantages & Disadvantages

  • Advantages: Immediate onset and precise plasma control.
  • Bypasses absorption barriers and first-pass metabolism.
  • Ideal for bolus or infusion dosing.
  • Disadvantages: Technically challenging.
  • Risk of extravasation, infection, or thrombosis.
  • Not suitable for oily or highly irritant drugs.

Equipment & Pharmacokinetics

  • Syringe: 0.5–1 mL (mice), 1–3 mL (rats).
  • Needle: 25–30G (mice tail), 23–27G (rats).
  • Onset: Immediate.
  • Bioavailability: 100%.
  • Recommended Dose: Mice ≤5 mL/kg bolus; Rats ≤10 mL/kg.
  • Inject slowly to prevent cardiac overload.
