Ocular Topical – Technique

  • Restrain animal gently to avoid stress.
  • Pull lower eyelid to form conjunctival pocket.
  • Instill 10–50 µL drops or ointment into conjunctival sac.
  • Avoid direct contact with cornea.
  • Allow blinking to distribute medication evenly.
  • Treat one eye; contralateral eye serves as control.

Note: Monitor for redness, discharge, irritation, or excessive blinking post-application.

Ocular Topical Administration

Overview

Ocular topical administration involves applying drug formulations directly into the eye for local therapeutic or irritation studies. It is commonly used in rabbits and rodents for ophthalmic testing.

Advantages & Disadvantages

  • Advantages: Direct local action in eye tissues.
  • Non-invasive and avoids first-pass metabolism.
  • Disadvantages: Limited systemic absorption.
  • Risk of irritation, tearing, or allergic response.
  • Requires careful technique to avoid corneal injury.

Pharmacokinetics

  • Local Effect: Rapid onset at ocular surface.
  • Absorption through conjunctival and corneal tissues.
  • Minimal systemic exposure in most cases.
  • Common in Draize eye irritation testing (rabbits).
  • Bioavailability depends on tear flow and formulation type.
