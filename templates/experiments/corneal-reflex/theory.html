Theory / Principle

Principle

Surface (topical) anaesthesia is produced when a local anaesthetic drug is applied directly to mucous membranes such as the cornea.

Local anaesthetics reversibly block voltage-gated Na⁺ channels in the free nerve endings of the cornea, preventing depolarization and propagation of action potentials.

This results in temporary loss of corneal sensation, observed as abolition of the corneal reflex.

Onset & Duration depend on:
• Drug concentration
• Lipid solubility
• pKa value
• Protein binding
