Result & Conclusion

Result

The test drug produced surface anaesthesia as evidenced by the loss of corneal reflex following topical application.

Lignocaine showed a faster onset and longer duration compared to Procaine. The reflex returned gradually, indicating that the anaesthetic action was reversible and localized.

  • Onset of Anaesthesia: 1.5–2 minutes
  • Duration of Action: 12–20 minutes
  • Reflex at Peak: Absent
  • Systemic Effects: Not observed

Conclusion

The experiment confirms that the test drug produces effective topical local anaesthetic (LA) activity by blocking voltage-gated sodium channels in corneal nerve endings.

Loss of corneal reflex without systemic effects demonstrates selective surface anaesthesia. This method is widely used to evaluate the potency and duration of ophthalmic local anaesthetics.
