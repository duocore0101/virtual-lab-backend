Theory / Principle

Definition of Bioassay

Bioassay is a method of estimating the potency or concentration of a substance by measuring its biological response in a living system and comparing it with a standard preparation.

Basic Principle

  • The biological response is proportional to the dose of the drug within a certain range.
  • The test substance and standard must act through the same mechanism and produce similar pharmacological effects.
  • Their dose-response curves should be parallel.

Relative Potency

The relative potency of the test sample is calculated by comparing its response with that of the standard under controlled conditions.

Types of Bioassay

  • Graded type: Response increases gradually (e.g., muscle contraction, blood pressure).
  • Quantal type: All-or-none response (e.g., death, convulsion).

Common Graded Bioassay Methods

  • Matching method
  • Bracketing method
  • Interpolation method
  • 3-point and 4-point bioassays

Importance of Bioassay

Bioassay is essential when chemical methods cannot accurately measure biological activity, especially for hormones, vaccines, antibiotics, and toxins.
