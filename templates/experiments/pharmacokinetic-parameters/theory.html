Basic Theory of Pharmacokinetics

Pharmacokinetics studies ADME — Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism and Excretion of drugs.

In a one-compartment IV bolus model, the body is treated as a single compartment with instantaneous distribution and first-order elimination.

Mathematical Model

Equation:

Cₚ = C₀ × e(–Kₑₗ × t)

Log Form (Semi-log plot):

log Cₚ = log C₀ – (Kₑₗ × t) / 2.303

Key Parameters & Significance (Part 1)

  • C₀: Initial concentration (extrapolated at t=0).
  • Kₑₗ (h⁻¹): Elimination rate constant from slope × –2.303.
  • t₁/₂: 0.693 / Kₑₗ (time for 50% elimination).

Key Parameters & Significance (Part 2)

  • Vd (L): Dose / C₀ — distribution extent.
  • AUC₀–∞: Total drug exposure (C₀ / Kₑₗ or trapezoidal rule).
  • Cl (L/h): Kₑₗ × Vd = Dose / AUC.
