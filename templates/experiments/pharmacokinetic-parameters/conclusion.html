Result & Conclusion

Result

From the given plasma concentration–time data of a 500 mg IV bolus dose (one-compartment model), the pharmacokinetic parameters were successfully calculated using both graphical (semi-log plot) and mathematical methods.

The semi-log plot produced a straight line, confirming that the drug follows first-order elimination kinetics. The elimination rate constant (Kₑₗ) was found to be 0.30 h⁻¹, and the biological half-life (t₁/₂) was 2.31 hours.

Conclusion

The apparent volume of distribution (Vd) was 20 L, suggesting that the drug distributes mainly in the extracellular fluid compartment. The total drug exposure (AUC₀–∞) was approximately 83–85 mg·h/L, and the clearance (Cl) was calculated as 6 L/h, indicating efficient elimination from the body.

The trapezoidal (non-compartmental) AUC value closely matched the model-dependent value, validating the accuracy of calculations and demonstrating that modern Excel-based methods provide results comparable to the manual graphical method.

Overall, the experiment successfully demonstrated the calculation and clinical interpretation of key pharmacokinetic parameters, which are essential for dosage regimen design, therapeutic drug monitoring, and optimization of drug therapy.
