Isolation of Frog's Heart and Assembly Setup

✖

Procedure

  1. Preparation of Frog (Pithing)
    • Select a healthy medium-sized frog.
    • Stun by sharp blow or brief ether/chloroform exposure.
    • Destroy brain using pithing needle via foramen magnum.
    • Double pith to destroy spinal cord.
    • Pin frog supine on frog board.
  2. Exposure of Heart
    • Make midline incision from abdomen to throat.
    • Cut sternum / pectoral girdle.
    • Open pericardium carefully.
    • Keep heart moist with Frog Ringer’s solution.
  3. Cannulation
    • Identify sinus venosus.
    • Pass ligature and insert venous cannula.
    • Secure cannula and cut aorta.
    • Connect to perfusion bottle (20–30 cm).
  4. Perfusion and Recording
    • Start perfusion at 30–40 drops/min.
    • Attach thread to ventricle tip.
    • Connect to Starling heart lever.
    • Record tracing on kymograph drum.

Precautions

  • Keep heart moist at all times.
  • Avoid air bubbles in cannula.
  • Aerate Ringer’s solution.
  • Maintain room temperature.
  • Follow ethical guidelines.
