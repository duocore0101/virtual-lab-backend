Theory / Principle

Frog Rectus Abdominis Preparation

The frog rectus abdominis muscle is a skeletal muscle preparation commonly used in pharmacological experiments. It contains nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs) that respond to cholinergic agonists.

Action of Acetylcholine

Acetylcholine (ACh) acts on the nicotinic receptors present on the rectus abdominis muscle. Activation of these receptors produces a slow and sustained contraction of the skeletal muscle fibers when the preparation is mounted in an organ bath.

Role of Physostigmine

Physostigmine (also called eserine) is an anticholinesterase drug. It inhibits the enzyme acetylcholinesterase (AChE), which normally breaks down acetylcholine in the synaptic region.

Potentiation of Acetylcholine Response

By inhibiting acetylcholinesterase, physostigmine allows acetylcholine to remain longer in the tissue. As a result, more ACh is available to bind to nicotinic receptors, leading to enhancement (potentiation) of the acetylcholine response. This causes an increase in the contractile response of the muscle.
