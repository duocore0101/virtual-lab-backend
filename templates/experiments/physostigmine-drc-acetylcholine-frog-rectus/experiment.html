Experimental Procedure

  1. Set up frog rectus abdominis muscle in organ bath and allow stabilization for 30–60 minutes.
  2. Add increasing volumes/concentrations of acetylcholine (e.g., 0.1, 0.2, 0.4, 0.8 ml of stock solution).
  3. Record contractions produced by each dose and wash the tissue after every response.
  4. Plot normal dose-response curve of ACh (log dose vs % maximal response or contraction height).
  5. Add physostigmine to the bath (e.g., 0.5–2 ml stock solution) and allow 5–10 minutes contact time.
  6. Repeat the ACh doses in presence of physostigmine and record new responses.
  7. Wash tissue and repeat the procedure if required.
