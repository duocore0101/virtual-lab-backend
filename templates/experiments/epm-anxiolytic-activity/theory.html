Theory – Elevated Plus Maze (EPM) Method

Principle

The Elevated Plus Maze (EPM) is a behavioral model used to evaluate anxiety and the anxiolytic activity of drugs in laboratory animals such as mice and rats.

The method is based on the natural conflict between the tendency of rodents to explore a new environment and their fear of open and elevated spaces. Anxiolytic drugs reduce this fear and increase exploratory behavior in open arms.

Open Arms

The open arms of the Elevated Plus Maze are unprotected and elevated, making them aversive to rodents under normal conditions.

  • Increased open arm entries: Indicates reduced anxiety and anxiolytic activity of the drug.
  • Increased time spent in open arms: Suggests enhanced exploratory behavior due to anxiolytic action.

Closed Arms

Closed arms are enclosed by high walls and provide a sense of safety to the animal, making them the preferred area for anxious rodents.

  • Increased closed arm entries: Indicates anxiety or lack of anxiolytic effect.
  • Prolonged time in closed arms: Reflects fear and anxiety-related behavior.

Significance

The Elevated Plus Maze is a simple, reliable, and widely accepted experimental model for screening anxiolytic drugs.

It helps in understanding the effects of drugs acting on the central nervous system and has significant relevance in the development and evaluation of anti-anxiety medications.
