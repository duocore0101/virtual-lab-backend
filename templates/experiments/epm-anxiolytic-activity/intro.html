To Study Anxiolytic Activity of Drugs Using Elevated Plus Maze (EPM) Method in Mice / Rats

Introduction

Anxiety is a behavioral response exhibited by animals when exposed to unfamiliar or stressful environments. In experimental pharmacology, animal models are widely used to evaluate drugs that modify anxiety-related behavior.

The Elevated Plus Maze (EPM) is a validated behavioral model used to study anxiety in rodents such as mice and rats. It is based on the natural aversion of rodents to open and elevated spaces. Anxiolytic drugs reduce this fear and increase exploratory behavior.

  • Open arms indicate reduced anxiety
  • Closed arms indicate anxiety-related behavior
  • Anxiolytic drugs increase open arm exploration
