Theory/Principle

Principle of the Rotarod Test

The rotarod test is used to evaluate motor coordination, balance, and skeletal muscle tone in laboratory animals. It is particularly useful for screening centrally acting skeletal muscle relaxants.

Mechanism of Skeletal Muscle Relaxants

Centrally acting skeletal muscle relaxants reduce muscle tone by acting on the central nervous system rather than directly on the neuromuscular junction.

Role of Diazepam

Diazepam enhances the inhibitory action of GABA at GABAA receptors, decreasing neuronal excitability and producing muscle relaxation.

Basis of Measurement

After administration of diazepam, the ability of mice to maintain balance on the rotating rod is reduced, leading to decreased fall-off time.

Interpretation

A reduction in latency to fall confirms impairment of motor coordination and indicates central skeletal muscle relaxant activity.
