Result & Conclusion

Result

Diazepam administered at a dose of 4 mg/kg (i.p.) produced a significant reduction in the fall-off time of mice on the rotarod apparatus when compared to pre-treatment values.

This indicates impairment of motor coordination and grip strength in the treated animals.

Conclusion

The present experiment confirms that diazepam possesses significant skeletal muscle relaxant activity. The reduction in endurance time on the rotarod apparatus demonstrates that diazepam acts centrally by enhancing GABA-mediated inhibitory neurotransmission in the central nervous system.
