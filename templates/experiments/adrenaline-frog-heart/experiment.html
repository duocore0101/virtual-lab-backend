To Effect of Adrenaline (Epinephrine) on Isolated Frog Heart Experiment

✖

Procedure

  1. Prepare the isolated perfused frog heart (pith frog, cannulate sinus venosus, attach to kymograph lever).
  2. Perfuse the heart with aerated Frog Ringer’s solution.
  3. Record normal baseline tracing (regular contractions, heart rate ~60–80 bpm).
  4. Prepare adrenaline solutions using serial dilutions.
  5. Administer adrenaline:
    • Inject small volumes (0.1–0.5 ml) of increasing concentrations via cannula.
    • Record kymograph tracing after each dose.
  6. Wash with normal Ringer’s solution to restore baseline.
  7. Optional: Pre-treat with propranolol (β-blocker), re-administer adrenaline, and observe reduced or blocked response.
