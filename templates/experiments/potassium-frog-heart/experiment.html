To Study Effect of Potassium Ions on Isolated Frog Heart

✖

Procedure

  1. Preparation (same as Calcium experiment):
    1. Pith the frog and expose the heart.
    2. Cannulate the sinus venosus using Syme’s cannula.
    3. Isolate the heart and mount it on the stand connected to the Starling’s heart lever.
    4. Perfuse with Normal Ringer’s solution to stabilize the beat.
  2. Experimental Protocol:
    1. Normal tracing: Record normal heart contractions for 1–2 minutes.
    2. Effect of Excess Potassium (Hyperkalemia):
      • Perfuse with High Potassium Ringer’s solution.
      • Observe decrease in heart rate and force.
      • Note diastolic arrest in relaxed (flaccid) state.
    3. Wash: Immediately wash with Normal Ringer’s solution.
    4. Effect of Lack of Potassium (Hypokalemia):
      • Perfuse with Potassium-Free Ringer’s solution.
      • Observe progressive rise in tone.
      • Note incomplete relaxation.
    5. Recovery: Reintroduce Normal Ringer’s solution.
✖

Observation
