To Study Effect of Potassium Ions on Isolated Frog Heart

Principle

Potassium (K⁺) is the primary intracellular cation and is the key determinant of the resting membrane potential (RMP) of cardiac muscle cells.

  • Resting Membrane Potential: The cardiac cell membrane is highly permeable to potassium ions. A high intracellular to extracellular potassium ratio maintains a negative RMP (approximately –90 mV).
  • Repolarization: Efflux of potassium ions is responsible for the repolarization phase (Phase 3) of the cardiac action potential, returning the heart muscle to its relaxed state.
  • Sensitivity of the Heart: The heart is extremely sensitive to changes in extracellular potassium concentration. Even slight deviations can markedly alter cardiac excitability, conduction, and rhythm.
