Result & Conclusion

Result

The selected serum biochemical parameter was successfully estimated using a semi-autoanalyser based on the specified wavelength and method.

The obtained value was compared with the standard normal range to determine its clinical significance.

Discussion

The recorded values from the instrument printout were analyzed and compared with established normal ranges.

Any deviation from the normal range may indicate underlying pathological conditions. For example, elevated glucose along with increased cholesterol levels may suggest metabolic syndrome or diabetes mellitus.

Enzyme parameters such as AST and ALT help assess liver function, while bilirubin levels assist in diagnosing jaundice and hepatic disorders.

Quality Control

The control values must lie within predefined control limits to validate the accuracy and reliability of the assay.

If control values fall outside the acceptable range, recalibration of the instrument and reagent verification are required before reporting patient results.
