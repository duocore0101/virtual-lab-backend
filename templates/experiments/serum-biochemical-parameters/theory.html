Theory / Principle

Principle of Semi-Autoanalyser

A semi-autoanalyser (semi-automated biochemistry analyser) is a compact laboratory instrument that automates the photometric measurement of absorbance while requiring manual steps such as sample/reagent pipetting and cuvette placement.

It operates on colorimetric or enzymatic methods using specific wavelengths (commonly 340, 505, 540, 578 nm) and maintains reactions at a controlled temperature (usually 37°C).

Applications & Features

Semi-autoanalysers are widely used in clinical laboratories for routine biochemical parameter estimation because they are cost-effective, require small sample volumes (5–100 µL), and provide accurate and reproducible results.

Results are calculated using pre-programmed factors or calibration curves. Common models include ERBA, Transasia, Bioevopeak, etc., which may be flow-cell or cuvette-based systems.
