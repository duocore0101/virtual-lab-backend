Theory / Principle

Charcoal Meal Method

Gastrointestinal motility can be evaluated using the charcoal meal method. In this method, a suspension of 10% charcoal in 5% gum acacia is administered orally to experimental animals.

The charcoal suspension acts as a marker that travels through the gastrointestinal tract along with intestinal contents. After a fixed time interval, the distance travelled by the charcoal meal through the intestine is measured.

The distance travelled by the charcoal marker reflects the level of gastrointestinal motility. Prokinetic drugs increase intestinal motility and therefore increase the distance travelled, whereas antimotility drugs decrease intestinal motility and reduce the distance travelled by the charcoal meal.
