Result & Conclusion

Result

The effect of drugs on gastrointestinal motility was evaluated using the charcoal meal method in experimental animals.

The distance travelled by the charcoal marker through the intestine was measured and expressed as percentage intestinal transit.

In the control group, the charcoal meal showed moderate intestinal transit. Administration of atropine decreased intestinal transit, indicating inhibition of gastrointestinal motility.

In contrast, administration of metoclopramide increased the percentage intestinal transit, demonstrating stimulation of gastrointestinal motility.

Conclusion

The effect of the test drug on gastrointestinal motility can be determined by comparing the percentage intestinal transit with that of the control group.

If the drug increases % intestinal transit, it is classified as a prokinetic drug.

If the drug decreases % intestinal transit, it is classified as an antimotility drug.

Note: Modern pharmacology laboratories often use virtual simulation experiments to study gastrointestinal motility and reduce the use of live animals.
