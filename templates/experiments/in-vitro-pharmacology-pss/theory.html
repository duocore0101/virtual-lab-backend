Introduction to In-Vitro Pharmacology and Physiological Salt Solutions

Introduction to In-Vitro Pharmacology

In-vitro pharmacology is the study of how drugs or chemical compounds interact with biological systems outside a living organism. The term “in-vitro” means “in glass” and refers to experiments performed in controlled laboratory environments.

Scope of In-Vitro Studies

In-vitro pharmacology focuses on studying drug effects on isolated cells, tissues, enzymes, receptors, or biochemical pathways to understand mechanisms of action, potency, efficacy, and toxicity at cellular or molecular levels.

Common In-Vitro Experimental Setups

  • Organ bath systems for isolated tissues
  • Multi-well plates for cell-based assays
  • Receptor and enzyme assay platforms

Methods and Assays

  • Cell-based assays for proliferation and toxicity
  • Receptor binding assays
  • Enzyme inhibition assays
  • Isolated tissue contraction studies
  • High-throughput screening (HTS)

Dose–Response Relationship

A dose–response curve plots drug concentration against biological response to determine:

  • EC50 – concentration producing 50% response
  • Emax – maximum effect
  • Comparison of agonists and antagonists

Role in Drug Discovery

  • Hit identification and screening
  • Lead optimization
  • Safety and toxicity assessment
  • Preclinical evaluation before animal studies

Physiological Salt Solutions (PSS)

Physiological Salt Solutions are artificial isotonic solutions designed to mimic extracellular fluid and maintain viability of isolated tissues during in-vitro experiments.

Functions of PSS

  • Provide essential ions
  • Maintain osmotic balance
  • Supply energy (glucose)
  • Maintain physiological pH

Types of Physiological Salt Solutions

  • Frog Ringer – amphibian tissues
  • Tyrode solution – mammalian smooth muscle
  • Krebs-Henseleit solution – innervated tissues
  • Ringer-Locke – isolated heart
  • De Jalon – rat uterus

Preparation and Precautions

  • Prepare fresh solutions daily
  • Add calcium salts last
  • Maintain pH around 7.4
  • Aerate with oxygen or carbogen
  • Maintain appropriate temperature
