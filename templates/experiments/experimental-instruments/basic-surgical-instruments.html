Basic Surgical Instruments Used in Experimental Pharmacology

Overview

In experimental pharmacology practicals, basic surgical instruments are essential for animal dissection, tissue isolation, cannulation, and minor surgical procedures performed on laboratory animals such as rats, mice, guinea pigs, frogs, or rabbits.

These instruments are commonly used during organ bath experiments, blood pressure recording, drug administration, and ex vivo tissue preparation. Most instruments are made of stainless steel for durability, corrosion resistance, and easy sterilization.

General Purpose

Standard surgical instrument sets used in pharmacology laboratories follow guidelines described in classical practical manuals (e.g., Kulkarni, Ghosh) and institutional animal ethics protocols. Proper handling ensures minimal tissue damage and reliable experimental outcomes.

Commonly Used Instruments and Their Purposes

  • Dissecting Forceps (Plain & Toothed): Fine-tipped instruments used to grasp tissues delicately (plain) or firmly (toothed). Commonly used for holding vessels, nerves, or isolating tissues such as guinea pig ileum.
  • Dissecting Scissors (Straight & Curved): Used for cutting tissues, membranes, and sutures. Curved scissors are preferred for deeper or blind dissections.
  • Scalpel with Blades: Used for making precise incisions on skin or organs. Disposable blades (No. 11 or No. 15) are commonly employed.
  • Arterial and Venous Cannulas: Polythene or glass tubes used for cannulating blood vessels (e.g., carotid artery for blood pressure recording, jugular vein for intravenous drug administration).
  • Bull Dog Clamps / Aneurysm Clips: Small vascular clamps used to temporarily occlude blood vessels without causing permanent damage.
  • Needle Holder: Instrument used for holding suturing needles during closure of incisions or securing cannulas.
  • Probes and Seekers: Blunt probes used for separating tissues; hooks or seekers help retract tissues gently.
  • Retractors: Used to hold back tissues and maintain exposure of the surgical field.
  • Dissecting Board (Frog Board): Wax or cork board used for pinning animals, especially during frog pithing or tissue dissection.

Other Accessories

  • Thread or silk sutures for tying vessels
  • Dissecting pins for fixation
  • Syringes and needles for injections
  • Saline, cotton swabs, and antiseptic solutions

Applications

  • Isolation of tissues for organ bath experiments
  • Cannulation for cardiovascular pharmacology studies
  • Minor surgical procedures in laboratory animals
  • Teaching experimental pharmacology practicals
  • Preclinical drug research and training
