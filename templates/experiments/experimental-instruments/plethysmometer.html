Plethysmometer (Digital Plethysmometer)

Overview

The Plethysmometer, commonly referred to as the Digital Plethysmometer or Paw Volume Meter, is a precision instrument used in experimental pharmacology to measure small changes in paw volume of rodents.

It is widely employed for evaluating anti-inflammatory drugs by quantifying paw edema induced by inflammatory agents such as carrageenan.

Principle

The plethysmometer operates based on Archimedes’ principle of water displacement. When the animal’s paw is dipped into a fluid-filled measuring cell up to a fixed reference point (usually the ankle joint), the volume of fluid displaced corresponds to the paw volume.

Modern digital plethysmometers use highly sensitive transducers to detect minute volume changes (resolution up to 0.01 ml), providing a direct digital readout. Older models used mercury, which has now been replaced for safety.

Effective anti-inflammatory drugs (e.g., diclofenac, indomethacin) significantly reduce edema volume compared to control animals.

Description of the Instrument

  • Measuring Cell: Small transparent tube or beaker filled with water or electrolyte solution, connected to a sensitive volume transducer.
  • Display: Digital display panel showing volume directly in milliliters (typical range 0–50 ml, accuracy ±0.01 ml).
  • Foot Switch / Pedal: Enables hands-free zeroing and measurement.
  • Features:
    • Tare/zero function
    • Calibration tube
    • Separate measuring cells for rats and mice
  • Commercial Models: Commonly available from manufacturers such as Ugo Basile and Panlab.
  • Advantages of Digital Versions: Mercury-free, highly precise, rapid, and easy to use.

Applications

  • Assessment of acute and chronic inflammation
  • Screening of anti-inflammatory and anti-edematous drugs
  • Evaluation of paw edema in experimental models
  • Preclinical pharmacological research
  • Teaching experimental pharmacology practicals
