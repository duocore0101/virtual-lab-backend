Digital Telethermometer

Overview

The Digital Telethermometer, also known as a Multi-Channel Digital Tele-Thermometer, is a specialized instrument used in experimental pharmacology for precise and continuous monitoring of body temperature in laboratory animals.

It is an essential tool for pyrogen testing to detect fever-inducing contaminants in injectable drugs, in accordance with pharmacopoeial standards such as USP, EP, and BP.

Principle

The digital telethermometer operates using thermistor- or thermocouple-based rectal probes inserted into the animal to accurately measure core body temperature. The instrument provides high resolution (approximately 0.1°C) and allows continuous temperature monitoring.

Multiple channels permit simultaneous monitoring of several animals. A rise in temperature of more than 0.5–1°C indicates a pyrogenic response, typically due to bacterial endotoxins such as lipopolysaccharides.

Description of the Instrument

  • Channels: Commonly 6-channel systems for monitoring six animals simultaneously; some models support 1–12 channels.
  • Probes: Flexible rectal probes with thermistors or temperature sensors and long leads to minimize animal disturbance.
  • Display: Digital LED/LCD display showing temperature (range typically 0–50°C or 20–45°C) with accuracy ±0.1°C.
  • Features:
    • Individual or combined display per channel
    • Calibration facility
    • Auto and manual operating modes
    • Built-in timer
  • Advanced Models: Software-based data logging, protocol compliance (USP/EP/BP), and printing capabilities.

Applications

  • Pyrogen testing of injectable pharmaceutical products
  • Monitoring body temperature during anesthesia
  • Hyperthermia and hypothermia studies
  • Physiological and toxicological research
  • Teaching experimental pharmacology practicals
