Elevated Plus Maze Apparatus

Overview

The Elevated Plus Maze (EPM) is one of the most widely used behavioral tests in experimental pharmacology for screening anxiolytic and anxiogenic drugs in rodents such as mice and rats.

It is a simple, unconditioned test based on the natural conflict between the animal’s drive to explore novel environments and its fear of open, elevated spaces.

Principle

Rodents naturally avoid open and elevated areas due to fear of falling, yet they are also motivated to explore new environments. The elevated plus maze creates a conflict between closed arms (safe, enclosed spaces) and open arms (exposed, aversive spaces).

Anxiolytic drugs increase the time spent and number of entries into open arms, whereas anxiogenic agents decrease these measures. The total number of arm entries reflects general locomotor activity, helping to distinguish true anxiolysis from sedation or stimulation.

Standard anxiolytics such as diazepam increase open-arm exploration without reducing overall locomotion.

Description of the Instrument

  • Structure: Plus-shaped (+) maze consisting of four arms (two open and two closed) intersecting at a central platform.
  • Elevation: Mounted on a stand 40–70 cm above the floor.
  • Dimensions (for mice):
    • Arm length: approximately 30–35 cm
    • Arm width: approximately 5–6 cm
    • Closed arm wall height: approximately 15–20 cm
    • Open arm ledges: 1–2 mm to prevent falling
  • Material: Opaque acrylic or Perspex, usually black or gray to minimize reflections.
  • Features: Tested in a quiet, dimly lit room; elevated design enhances anxiety-related behavior.
  • Modern Versions: Overhead video cameras and automated tracking software (e.g., ANY-maze) for precise analysis of arm entries, time spent, head dips, and stretch-attend postures.
  • Variants: Larger rat-sized mazes and the zero-maze (circular alternative without a central platform).

Applications

  • Screening of anxiolytic and anxiogenic drugs
  • Assessment of anxiety-related behavior
  • Evaluation of sedative or stimulant side effects
  • Behavioral pharmacology research
  • Teaching experimental pharmacology practicals
