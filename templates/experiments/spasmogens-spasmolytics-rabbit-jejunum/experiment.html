Effect of Spasmogens and Spasmolytics on Isolated Rabbit Jejunum

✖

Observation Table – Rabbit Jejunum Experiment

S. No Drug Administered Dose Observation (Height of Response) Inference
1 Normal Saline 0.1 ml No change (Constant waves) Control / Baseline
2 Acetylcholine (ACh) 10 µg Increased tone & amplitude (≈25 mm) Spasmogenic effect via M3 receptors
3 Barium Chloride 100 µg Increased tone (sustained spasm) Spasmogenic – Direct muscle depolarization
4 Atropine 10 µg Decreased tone (Relaxation) Spasmolytic – Antimuscarinic
5 Adrenaline 10 µg Decreased tone & frequency Spasmolytic – Sympathomimetic
6 Atropine + ACh Both No response (or reduced < 5 mm) Atropine blocked action of ACh (Competitive antagonism)
✖

Experimental Procedure

  1. Sacrifice the rabbit by air embolism or cervical dislocation.
  2. Open the abdomen and isolate a 2–3 cm segment of jejunum.
  3. Flush lumen gently with Tyrode's solution.
  4. Mount tissue in organ bath with Tyrode's solution at 37°C with aeration.
  5. Attach to isotonic lever with ~1 g tension and equilibrate 30–45 minutes.
  6. Record normal spontaneous pendular movements.
  7. Add drugs cumulatively (contact time 1–2 minutes with washing).
  8. Spasmogens: Acetylcholine or Barium Chloride.
  9. Spasmolytics: Atropine, Adrenaline or Papaverine.
  10. Pre-incubate with atropine before ACh for antagonism study.
  11. Record changes in tone, amplitude and frequency.
