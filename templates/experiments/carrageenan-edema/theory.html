Theory / Principle

Principle of Carrageenan-Induced Paw Edema

Subplantar injection of carrageenan (a sulfated polysaccharide from seaweed) into the rat hind paw induces acute inflammation.

Pathophysiology of Inflammation

The inflammation is characterized by increased vascular permeability, neutrophil infiltration, and release of inflammatory mediators.

Early Phase (0–1 Hour)

The early phase is mediated by:

  • Histamine
  • Serotonin
  • Bradykinin

Late Phase (1–6 Hours)

The late phase is mainly mediated by:

  • Prostaglandins (PGE2)
  • Cytokines
  • COX-2 induction

Basis of Measurement & Drug Action

This mediator release leads to paw swelling (edema), measured as increase in paw volume using a plethysmometer.

Anti-inflammatory drugs reduce edema by inhibiting these mediators, particularly in the late phase, indicating COX inhibition.
