Result & Conclusion

Result

The paw volume was recorded before and after carrageenan injection in control, standard, and test drug-treated groups using a digital plethysmometer.

Diclofenac / Indomethacin (standard drug) significantly inhibited paw edema (50–70% at peak), mainly in the late phase (3–4 hours), confirming cyclooxygenase (COX) inhibition.

The test drug showed a reduction in paw edema compared to the control group, indicating anti-inflammatory activity.

Conclusion

Carrageenan-induced paw edema is a well-established model for evaluating acute anti-inflammatory activity.

Greater inhibition during the late phase suggests prostaglandin-mediated action similar to NSAIDs, indicating possible COX inhibition.

If the test drug shows dose-dependent and significant reduction in paw edema, it possesses anti-inflammatory activity.

Lack of significant effect may indicate absence of acute anti-inflammatory potential or involvement of a different mechanism of action.
