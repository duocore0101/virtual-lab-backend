One-Way ANOVA

One-Way ANOVA

When to use: Compare means of 3 or more independent groups (e.g., control + standard + test doses).

Common Experiments

  • Carrageenan-induced paw edema (anti-inflammatory)
  • Acetic acid writhing / Eddy’s hot plate (analgesic)
  • Alloxan/STZ-induced diabetes (antidiabetic – blood glucose)
  • Diuretic activity, anti-ulcer ulcer index, pentobarbitone sleeping time, etc.

Post-hoc Tests (Mandatory after significant ANOVA)

Conclusion

“One-way ANOVA followed by Dunnett’s multiple comparison test showed that low dose, high dose and standard drug produced statistically significant reduction in blood glucose level compared to diabetic control (p < 0.001). The test drug therefore possesses significant and dose-dependent antidiabetic activity.”
