To Study and Evaluate Analgesic Activity by Hot Plate Method

Principle

Heat serves as a thermal nociceptive stimulus. The animal (mouse or rat) is placed on a heated surface (usually 55 ± 0.5°C), and the latency (time) to show pain responses such as paw licking, paw withdrawal, shaking, or jumping is measured.

Centrally acting analgesics delay these responses by acting on higher brain centers or spinal pathways.
