{% load static %} Effect of Acetylcholine on Frog’s Heart – Experiment

To study the effect of acetylcholine on frog’s heart

✖

Procedure

  1. Preparation of the Heart
    • Pith the frog to destroy brain and spinal cord.
    • Expose heart by opening thoracic cavity.
    • Insert Syme’s cannula into sinus venosus and start perfusion.
    • Isolate heart and mount on stand.
    • Attach ventricle apex to Starling’s heart lever.
  2. Experimental Protocol
    • Record normal cardiogram with Normal Ringer.
    • Administer excess calcium and record changes.
    • Wash with Normal Ringer.
    • Switch to Calcium-Free Ringer and observe changes.
    • Reintroduce Normal Ringer for recovery.
✖

Observation

Condition Observation (Contraction Pattern) Effect on Rate (Chronotropy) Effect on Force (Inotropy)
Normal Ringer Regular rhythmic beats with distinct systole and diastole Normal Normal
Mild Increase (Calcium ions) Height of contraction increases Positive (+ve) Positive (+ve)
High Increase (Calcium ions) Heart fails to relax completely, baseline rises Irregular / Stops Systolic Arrest
Calcium Free / Low Height of contraction decreases progressively Negative (−ve) Negative (−ve)
