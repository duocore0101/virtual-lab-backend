Result & Conclusion

Result

The experiment demonstrates the effect of different osmotic solutions on the frog intestine by observing changes in volume due to osmosis.

The hypotonic solution caused shrinkage of the intestinal segment, the isotonic solution showed no visible change, and the hypertonic magnesium sulphate solution produced marked swelling (distension).

Conclusion

Interpretation of the Graph

  1. Hypotonic Solution (0.9% NaCl / Dilute Saline):
    • Observation: Negative bar indicating shrinkage.
    • Mechanism: The solution inside the intestine is less concentrated than frog body fluids. Water moves by osmosis from lumen/blood into surrounding tissues.
    • Result: The intestinal segment becomes shriveled.
  2. Isotonic Solution (Frog Ringer's Solution):
    • Observation: Zero change.
    • Mechanism: The solution has the same osmolarity as body fluids. No net movement of water occurs.
    • Result: The intestinal segment retains its normal tone and size.
  3. Hypertonic Solution (27% Magnesium Sulphate – Saline Purgative):
    • Observation: Positive bar showing swelling/distension.
    • Mechanism: High concentration of non-absorbable ions (MgSO₄) creates high osmotic pressure, drawing water from blood/tissues into the intestinal lumen.
    • Result: Fluid accumulation stretches the intestinal wall. In living systems, this stretch increases peristalsis and produces a laxative (purgative) effect.
