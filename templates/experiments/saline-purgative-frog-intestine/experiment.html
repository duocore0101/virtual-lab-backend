To study Effect of Saline Purgatives on Frog Intestine

✖

Procedure

  1. Pith the frog (destroy brain and spinal cord humanely) and pin it ventral side up on the frog board.
  2. Open the abdominal cavity by a midline incision and expose the small intestine.
  3. Carefully isolate a segment of small intestine (ileum preferred).
  4. Divide the intestine into three equal compartments by tightly tying threads (use different colors).
  5. Using a syringe, inject:
    • Compartment 1: 0.2 mL hypotonic saline.
    • Compartment 2: 0.2 mL hypertonic MgSO₄ (saline purgative).
    • Compartment 3: 0.2 mL Frog Ringer's solution.
  6. Leave the preparation for 20–30 minutes.
  7. Observe and note changes in volume/diameter of each compartment.
✖

Observation

Typical results after 20–30 minutes:

Compartment Solution Injected Observation Reason
1 Hypotonic (0.45% NaCl) Shrunken (reduced volume) Water moves from lumen to tissues (osmosis).
2 Hypertonic (27% MgSO₄) Swollen (increased volume) Water moves from tissues to lumen → mimics purgative action by increasing luminal fluid.
3 Isotonic (Frog Ringer's) No change Equilibrium; no net water movement.
