Taming and Hypnosis Potentiating Effect of Chlorpromazine

✖

Procedure

  1. Select healthy mice.
  2. Place the mice individually inside Cooke’s pole climbing apparatus.
  3. The apparatus contains a floor grid carrying electric current and a pole suspended from the top of the chamber.
  4. Train mice to escape electric shock by climbing the pole (non-conditioned response).
  5. Train mice to climb the pole when danger signal (buzzer/light) occurs before shock (conditioned avoidance response).
  6. Keep mice no.1 as control.
  7. Inject chlorpromazine (4 mg/kg i.p.) to mice no.2.
  8. Observe conditioned and unconditioned responses.
  9. Chlorpromazine blocks conditioned avoidance response but does not block escape response to electric shock.
