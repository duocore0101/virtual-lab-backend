Theory

Role of Laboratory Animals

Laboratory animals are essential in experimental pharmacology for evaluating drug efficacy, safety, toxicity, and mechanisms of action before clinical use in humans.

Commonly Used Species

Frequently used laboratory animals include mice, rats, guinea pigs, hamsters, frogs, dogs, and cats. Each species is selected based on experimental objectives and physiological relevance.

Basis for Animal Selection

Selection depends on factors such as similarity to human physiology, ease of handling, reproductive rate, availability, cost, and existing background research data.

Ethical Principles – 3Rs

The use of laboratory animals follows the 3Rs principle: Replacement, Reduction, and Refinement, ensuring humane and responsible research.

Regulatory Guidelines

Animal experimentation is regulated by bodies such as CPCSEA (India) and IACUC (International), which enforce ethical standards and animal welfare.
