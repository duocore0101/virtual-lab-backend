Concept of pA₂ and Schild Plot

The pA₂ value (coined by Heinz Otto Schild, 1947) is defined as the negative logarithm (base 10) of the molar concentration of a competitive antagonist that produces a dose ratio (DR) of 2.

It represents the affinity of the antagonist for the receptor. Higher pA₂ value indicates greater potency.

In competitive antagonism:
• Parallel rightward shift of dose-response curve
• No change in maximum response (Emax)
• Schild plot slope ≈ –1

Rat Anococcygeus Muscle Model

The rat anococcygeus muscle is a thin smooth muscle richly supplied with α₁-adrenoceptors, making it ideal for studying α₁-antagonism.

Noradrenaline (NA) produces dose-dependent contraction by activating α₁ receptors.

Prazosin, a selective α₁ antagonist, competitively blocks noradrenaline and shifts the dose-response curve to the right.

Schild Equation:
log(DR – 1) = log[B] + pA₂
