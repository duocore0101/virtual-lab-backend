Result & Conclusion

Result

The pA₂ value of prazosin on rat anococcygeus muscle against noradrenaline was found to be:

pA₂ = 6.10

The observed antagonism showed:

  • Parallel rightward shift of dose-response curve
  • No reduction in maximum response (Emax)
  • Schild plot slope ≈ –1

Conclusion

The experiment confirms that prazosin acts as a competitive antagonist at α₁-adrenoceptors in rat anococcygeus muscle.

The calculated pA₂ value (6.10) reflects the affinity of prazosin for α₁ receptors. The linear Schild plot with slope approximately –1 validates the principles of competitive antagonism.

This method is widely used in pharmacology to determine antagonist potency and receptor affinity.
