{% load static %} Effect of Calcium Ions on Frog’s Heart – Experiment

To study the effect of varying concentrations of calcium ions on isolated frog heart

✖

Procedure

  1. Pithing: Pith the frog to destroy the brain and spinal cord.
  2. Dissection: Open the thoracic cavity and expose the heart.
  3. Cannulation:
    • Identify the sinus venosus.
    • Insert Syme’s cannula and secure with thread.
    • Start perfusion immediately with Normal Ringer’s solution.
  4. Isolation: Remove the heart with cannula and mount on stand.
  5. Attachment: Connect ventricular apex to Starling’s heart lever.
  6. Normal Tracing: Record baseline contractions using Normal Ringer’s.
  7. Excess Calcium:
    • Inject CaCl₂ (0.2–0.5 ml) or switch to high-calcium Ringer’s.
    • Record changes in rate and amplitude.
  8. Wash: Restore baseline using Normal Ringer’s.
  9. Calcium-Free: Switch to calcium-free Ringer’s and observe.
  10. Recovery: Reintroduce Normal Ringer’s solution.
✖

Observation

Condition Observation (Contraction Pattern) Effect on Rate (Chronotropy) Effect on Force (Inotropy)
Normal Ringer Regular rhythmic beats with distinct systole and diastole Normal Normal
Mild Increase (Ca²⁺) Height of contraction increases Positive (+ve) Positive (+ve)
High Increase (Ca²⁺) Heart fails to relax completely; baseline rises Irregular / Stops Systolic Arrest
