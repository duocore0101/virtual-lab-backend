Conclusion

The anticonvulsant activity of the test drug was evaluated using Maximal Electroshock Seizure (MES) and Pentylenetetrazol (PTZ) induced seizure models in experimental animals.

In the MES method, the standard drug Phenytoin significantly reduced hind limb tonic extension, indicating protection against generalized tonic–clonic seizures. The test drug also showed appreciable anticonvulsant activity when compared to the control group.

In the PTZ-induced seizure model, the standard drug Diazepam provided complete protection against clonic seizures. The test drug produced a significant delay in seizure onset and partial protection, demonstrating its efficacy against absence and clonic seizures.

Thus, it can be concluded that the test drug possesses significant anticonvulsant activity, as evidenced by its protective effects in both MES and PTZ experimental models.
