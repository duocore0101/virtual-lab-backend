Requirements: Anticonvulsant Activity by MES or PTZ Method

Common Requirements

  • Healthy experimental animals
  • Proper animal housing and acclimatization facilities
  • Stopwatch
  • Individual observation cages
  • Normal saline / distilled water (vehicle)

A) Requirements for MES Method

Animals

  • Swiss albino mice (18–30 g) or
  • Wistar rats (150–250 g)
  • Grouped (n = 6 per group)

Equipment

  • Electroconvulsiometer
  • Corneal or ear clip electrodes

Electrical Parameters

  • Mice: 50–60 mA, 0.2 sec duration, 50–60 Hz
  • Rats: 150 mA, 0.2 sec duration

Drugs

  • Standard drug: Phenytoin (20–30 mg/kg, i.p.)
  • Test drug (various doses)
  • Vehicle: Normal saline / distilled water

Other Materials

  • Electrolyte gel or saline
  • Soft cloth for gentle restraint

B) Requirements for PTZ Method

Animals

  • Swiss albino mice (18–30 g)
  • Grouped (n = 6 per group)

Drugs

  • Convulsant agent: Pentylenetetrazol (PTZ) 80–90 mg/kg, s.c.
  • Standard drug: Diazepam (4–5 mg/kg, i.p.)
  • Test drug (various doses)
  • Vehicle: Normal saline

Other Materials

  • Stopwatch
  • Individual transparent observation cages
  • Syringes and needles

Ethical Considerations

  • Approval from Institutional Animal Ethics Committee (IAEC)
  • Minimum number of animals should be used
  • Animal discomfort and stress should be minimized
  • Proper post-experimental care should be provided
