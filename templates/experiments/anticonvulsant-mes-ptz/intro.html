Anticonvulsant Activity by Maximal Electroshock Seizure (MES) or PTZ Method

Introduction

Epilepsy is a chronic neurological disorder characterized by recurrent seizures due to abnormal electrical activity in the brain. Experimental animal models are widely used to evaluate the anticonvulsant potential of drugs.

The Maximal Electroshock Seizure (MES) method and the Pentylenetetrazol (PTZ) induced seizure method are standard screening models used in pharmacology to assess anticonvulsant activity.

  • MES method is used to identify drugs effective against generalized tonic–clonic (grand mal) seizures by observing abolition of hind limb tonic extension.
  • PTZ method is used to evaluate drugs effective against clonic, myoclonic, and absence (petit mal) seizures by measuring delay or prevention of convulsions.
  • Together, these models help in determining the spectrum of anticonvulsant activity of a test drug.
