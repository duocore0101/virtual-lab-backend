Conclusion – Anti-Catatonic (Anti-Cataleptic) Activity

Result

Administration of haloperidol produced marked catalepsy in rats/mice, as evidenced by prolonged maintenance of imposed posture. The cataleptic duration was recorded using standard observation methods.

Following administration of the test drug, a reduction in cataleptic time was observed when compared to the control group.

Interpretation

Haloperidol-induced catalepsy is primarily due to blockade of dopamine D2 receptors in the nigrostriatal pathway, leading to extrapyramidal motor impairment.

The observed reduction in cataleptic duration after drug treatment indicates restoration of dopaminergic–cholinergic balance in the central nervous system.

Conclusion

The test drug exhibited significant anti-catatonic (anti-cataleptic) activity by reducing haloperidol-induced catalepsy in experimental animals.

Hence, the drug may possess anti-parkinsonian properties or a lower liability for extrapyramidal side effects, making it pharmacologically safer for clinical use.
