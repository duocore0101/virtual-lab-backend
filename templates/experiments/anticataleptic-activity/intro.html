Introduction

Catalepsy is a state of immobility characterized by maintenance of imposed posture and muscular rigidity. In experimental pharmacology, catalepsy is commonly used as a behavioral model to evaluate the motor side effects produced by antipsychotic drugs.

The anti-catatonic (anti-cataleptic) activity test is used to assess the ability of drugs to reduce or reverse drug-induced catalepsy in laboratory animals such as rats or mice.

This virtual experiment introduces the concept of haloperidol-induced catalepsy and provides an overview of how anti-catatonic activity is evaluated using standard experimental methods.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the concept of catalepsy in experimental pharmacology
  • Learn the purpose of anti-catatonic activity studies
  • Become familiar with haloperidol-induced catalepsy models
  • Prepare for experimental evaluation using bar and block tests
