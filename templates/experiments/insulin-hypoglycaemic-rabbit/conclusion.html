Result & Conclusion

Result

Insulin produced a significant hypoglycaemic effect in the rabbit model.

After subcutaneous administration, blood glucose levels decreased markedly, with the maximum fall (approximately 40–60%) observed at 1.5–3 hours post-injection.

Thereafter, glucose levels gradually increased due to physiological counter-regulatory mechanisms (glucagon, adrenaline, cortisol).

These findings confirm the potent hypoglycaemic action of insulin in a normal fasted rabbit.

Conclusion / Inference

Insulin induces dose-dependent hypoglycaemia by enhancing peripheral glucose utilization and suppressing hepatic glucose output.

The observed time course demonstrates:

  • Onset within 30–60 minutes
  • Peak effect at 1–3 hours
  • Duration of action approximately 4–6 hours (for regular insulin)

Thus, the experiment successfully demonstrates the pharmacological action of insulin in lowering blood glucose levels.
