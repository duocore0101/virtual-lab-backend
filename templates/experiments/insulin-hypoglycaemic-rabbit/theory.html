Theory / Principle

Introduction to Insulin

Insulin is a peptide hormone secreted by the β-cells of the pancreas. It plays a vital role in maintaining normal blood glucose levels in the body.

Mechanism of Action

Insulin lowers blood glucose by:

  • Promoting glucose uptake into muscle and adipose tissue via GLUT4 transporters
  • Stimulating glycogenesis in liver and muscle
  • Inhibiting glycogenolysis
  • Inhibiting gluconeogenesis in the liver

Effect in Fasted Rabbit

In a healthy fasted rabbit, subcutaneous injection of insulin induces hypoglycaemia (fall in blood glucose level).

The extent and duration of this fall indicate insulin's potency and time course of action.

Basis of Measurement

Blood glucose is measured at:

  • Baseline (0 hour)
  • 1 hour
  • 2 hours
  • 3 hours
  • 4–5 hours post-injection

A progressive fall followed by gradual recovery is observed.

Calculations and Interpretation

1. Percentage Fall:

% Fall = (Baseline − Glucose at time t) × 100 / Baseline

2. Area Under Curve (AUC):

Calculated using trapezoidal rule on glucose-time graph.

3. Maximum Hypoglycaemic Response:

Lowest glucose value and time to reach it.

4. Duration of Action:

Time taken for glucose to return within 10–20% of baseline.
