Anaesthesia and Euthanasia Techniques

ANESTHESIA TECHNIQUES IN EXPERIMENTAL ANIMALS

Anesthesia in laboratory animals, particularly rodents such as mice and rats, is essential for surgical procedures, imaging, and experimental interventions in pharmacology. Protocols must balance immobilization, analgesia, and rapid recovery while minimizing physiological interference.

Current guidelines emphasize inhalant anesthesia as the gold standard because it allows precise control of anesthesia depth and quick recovery. Injectable protocols are also used for short procedures.

1. Inhalant Anesthesia (Preferred Method)

Isoflurane is the most widely used inhalant anesthetic agent. It allows rapid induction and recovery with minimal metabolism.

  • Rapid induction and recovery
  • Precise control using vaporizer
  • Minimal drug interference in pharmacology studies
  • Uses induction chamber and nose mask

Protocol

  • Induction: 3–5% isoflurane in oxygen
  • Maintenance: 1–3%
  • No fasting required for rodents

2. Injectable Anesthesia

Injectable anesthesia is commonly used for short or non-recovery procedures. It has a narrower safety margin compared with inhalant anesthesia.

  • Ketamine + Xylazine combination widely used
  • Mice: Ketamine 80–100 mg/kg + Xylazine 5–10 mg/kg
  • Rats: Ketamine 75–100 mg/kg + Xylazine 5–10 mg/kg
  • Duration: 20–60 minutes surgical anesthesia

Monitoring and Support

  • Respiratory rate and heart rate monitoring
  • Body temperature maintenance (37–38°C)
  • Reflex monitoring (toe pinch reflex)
  • Use warming devices and fluid therapy when required

EUTHANASIA TECHNIQUES IN EXPERIMENTAL ANIMALS

Euthanasia in laboratory animals must ensure a rapid, painless, and distress-free death while following ethical guidelines. The AVMA Guidelines for the Euthanasia of Animals (2020) provide the international standard.

Methods must minimize stress, avoid contamination of tissues, and ensure death is confirmed with a secondary method.

1. Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) Overdose

Carbon dioxide euthanasia is the most common method used in rodents. It is widely accepted because of its reliability and ease of use.

  • Use compressed CO₂ gas cylinders only
  • Gradual fill rate: 30–70% chamber volume per minute
  • Maintain gas flow after respiratory arrest
  • Confirm death using secondary method

2. Injectable Barbiturate Overdose

Barbiturate overdose is another commonly used euthanasia technique. It is especially useful when CO₂ may interfere with tissue analysis.

  • Pentobarbital sodium dose: 150–200 mg/kg
  • Usually administered via intraperitoneal injection
  • Produces deep anesthesia followed by respiratory arrest

Other Physical Methods

  • Cervical dislocation for rodents under 200 g
  • Decapitation using guillotine when brain tissue required

Unacceptable Methods

  • Chloroform or ether
  • Nitrogen or argon without anesthesia
  • Rapid freezing without anesthesia
