Marginal Ear Vein – Procedure

  • Restrain the rabbit using a restrainer or towel wrap (“burrito” method).
  • Clip hair on dorsal ear surface and clean with alcohol.
  • Apply local anesthetic cream and warm the ear to dilate vessels.
  • Identify the marginal ear vein along the pinna edge.
  • Puncture vein with 23–26G butterfly needle or cannula.
  • Collect blood by gravity or gentle aspiration.

Post-Procedure: Apply firm pressure for 2–5 minutes to achieve hemostasis. Monitor ear for bruising or necrosis.

Marginal Ear Vein / Artery Blood Collection

Overview

Marginal ear vein blood collection is a non-terminal survival procedure primarily used in rabbits for obtaining small to moderate blood volumes during pharmacokinetic, toxicological, and biomarker studies. The marginal vein is preferred over the central ear artery to reduce complications such as hematoma formation.

Key Guidelines and Welfare Considerations

  • Species: Primarily rabbits (e.g., New Zealand White)
  • Blood Volume: Typically 0.5–5 ml per collection
  • Advantages: Suitable for serial sampling
  • Risks: Vein collapse, hematoma, ear tip necrosis
  • Refinement: Local anesthesia, warming, gentle handling

Blood Volume Guidelines

  • Total circulating volume: ~55–70 ml/kg
  • Single sample: Up to 10% of circulating volume
  • Repeat sampling: 7.5–10% every 2–4 weeks
  • Microsampling preferred for refinement
