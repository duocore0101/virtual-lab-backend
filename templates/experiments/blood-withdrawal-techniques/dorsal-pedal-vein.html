Dorsal Pedal Vein – Procedure

  • Restrain the animal manually or using a restrainer.
  • Extend the hind limb to expose the dorsal foot.
  • Clip or shave the dorsal foot and clean with alcohol.
  • Apply gentle proximal pressure to dilate the vein.
  • Puncture the dorsal metatarsal vein using a fine needle.
  • Collect blood as drops into a capillary tube.

Post-Procedure: Apply firm pressure until bleeding stops and monitor for bruising or lameness.

Dorsal Pedal Vein Blood Collection

Overview

The dorsal pedal (metatarsal) vein is a superficial vein on the dorsal surface of the hind foot. It is a refined non-terminal technique used for very small-volume serial blood sampling in rodents.

Key Guidelines and Welfare Considerations

  • Species: Mice, rats, guinea pigs
  • Blood Volume: 0.05–0.2 ml (mice), up to 0.5 ml (rats)
  • Anesthesia: Not usually required
  • Advantages: Low stress, conscious sampling
  • Ethics: Strong refinement per NC3Rs

Advantages

  • Allows frequent repeat sampling
  • Good alternative to tail vein methods
  • Minimal tissue trauma
