Tail Snip – Procedure

  • Provide anesthesia or analgesia (recommended for adults).
  • Warm the tail if required and disinfect the tail tip.
  • Use sterile scissors or blade to snip 1–2 mm of tail tip.
  • Massage tail gently and collect blood drops into tube.
  • Apply pressure or styptic to stop bleeding.

Post-Procedure: Monitor healing closely; cauterize if required. Observe for signs of infection or delayed healing.

Tail Snip (Tail Clip) Blood Collection

Overview

Tail snip blood collection involves transecting the distal tail tip to obtain blood drops. It is primarily used in neonatal or young mice for genotyping, and is less preferred in adults due to pain and infection risks compared to needle-based methods.

Key Guidelines and Welfare Considerations

  • Species: Mainly neonatal or young mice
  • Blood Volume: 0.05–0.2 ml (drops)
  • Advantages: Quick method for neonates
  • Risks: Pain, infection, neuroma formation
  • Guidelines: Analgesia required; restricted per IACUC/NC3Rs

Ethical Notes

  • Prefer needle-based tail vein puncture in adults
  • Limited repeat sampling due to tail shortening
  • Strict aseptic technique mandatory
  • Use only when scientifically justified
