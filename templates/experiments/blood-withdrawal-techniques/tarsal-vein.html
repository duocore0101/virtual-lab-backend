Tarsal Vein – Procedure

  • Restrain the animal manually (scruff for mice/rats; towel wrap for guinea pigs).
  • Clip or shave hair over the hind limb and clean with alcohol.
  • Warm the limb or apply topical vasodilator to dilate the vein.
  • Extend the hind leg and occlude the vein proximally above the hock.
  • Puncture the vein transversely near the tarsal region.
  • Collect blood by gravity or gentle aspiration.

Post-Procedure: Apply firm pressure for 1–3 minutes to achieve hemostasis. Monitor for bruising or lameness and alternate limbs during repeat sampling.

Tarsal Vein Blood Collection

Overview

The tarsal vein (lateral or medial saphenous vein) is a superficial vein located around the tarsal (hock) region of the hind limb. It is one of the most refined non-terminal blood collection sites in rodents and guinea pigs for serial sampling in experimental pharmacology.

Key Guidelines and Welfare Considerations

  • Primary Species: Mice, rats, guinea pigs
  • Blood Volume: 0.1–0.5 ml per sample (up to 1 ml in larger animals)
  • Anesthesia: Not usually required (conscious sampling)
  • Advantages: Low stress, suitable for frequent serial sampling
  • Ethics: Strong refinement under the 3Rs principle

Needle and Collection Details

  • 23–25G needle for mice
  • 21–23G needle for rats and guinea pigs
  • Capillary tubes or microtainers for microsampling
  • Avoid excessive suction to prevent hemolysis
