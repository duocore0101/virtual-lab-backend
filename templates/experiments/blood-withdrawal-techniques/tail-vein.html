Tail Vein – Procedure

  • Restrain animal using a tube restrainer or manual scruff/towel wrap.
  • Warm the tail gently (lamp, warm pad, or water at ~40°C).
  • Clean tail with alcohol and identify the lateral vein.
  • Puncture the vein distally with a shallow angle (10–30°).
  • Collect blood by gentle aspiration or capillary action.

Post-Procedure: Apply firm pressure for 1–2 minutes to achieve hemostasis. Monitor tail for bruising or bleeding.

Tail Vein Blood Collection

Overview

Tail vein blood collection is a common non-terminal survival technique used in mice and rats for serial sampling in pharmacokinetic, glucose-monitoring, and biomarker studies. Lateral tail veins are preferred to minimize complications.

Key Guidelines and Welfare Considerations

  • Species: Mice and rats
  • Blood Volume: Mice 0.05–0.2 ml; Rats 0.2–1.5 ml
  • Advantages: Serial sampling, minimal equipment
  • Risks: Vein collapse, bruising, hemolysis
  • Refinement: Warming, acclimatization, limited attempts

Needle Selection

  • Mice: 25–27G needle or butterfly
  • Rats: 23–25G needle
  • Avoid scalpel cuts; needle puncture preferred
  • Limit to ≤3 attempts per session
