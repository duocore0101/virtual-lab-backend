Theory

Introduction to Blood Withdrawal

Blood withdrawal is a fundamental laboratory technique in experimental pharmacology used to obtain blood samples for biochemical, hematological, and pharmacokinetic analysis in laboratory animals.

Purpose of Blood Collection

Blood samples are required for estimation of drug levels, metabolites, biomarkers, hormones, and safety parameters. Proper blood collection ensures reliable experimental data and reproducibility.

Types of Blood Collection Methods

Blood withdrawal techniques are classified as non-terminal (tail vein, saphenous vein, marginal ear vein) and terminal methods (cardiac puncture, posterior vena cava), depending on volume required and study design.

Animal Welfare & Ethical Considerations

All blood collection procedures must follow ethical principles including the 3Rs – Replacement, Reduction, and Refinement. Stress, pain, and blood volume limits must be strictly controlled.

Guidelines and Safety

Blood withdrawal should be performed by trained personnel under institutional and international guidelines (NC3Rs, IACUC, AVMA). Proper restraint, aseptic technique, and anesthesia (when required) are essential for animal safety.
