Retro-Orbital Sinus – Procedure

  • Induce deep general anesthesia and confirm absence of pedal reflex.
  • Restrain animal and gently protrude the eyeball.
  • Insert a plain or heparinized glass capillary tube at the medial canthus.
  • Direct tube posteriorly at a 30–45° angle and rotate gently.
  • Allow blood to flow by capillary action into the tube.

Post-Procedure: Apply ophthalmic ointment and monitor closely for ocular complications such as hemorrhage or inflammation.

Retro-Orbital Sinus Blood Collection

Overview

Retro-orbital sinus blood collection is a technique historically used in laboratory rodents to obtain moderate to large blood volumes. Due to significant animal welfare concerns, its use is now highly restricted and discouraged in favor of refined alternatives.

Current Guidelines and Welfare Considerations

  • Restriction: Increasingly prohibited for survival use
  • Anesthesia: Mandatory deep general anesthesia
  • Risks: Ocular trauma, hemorrhage, neural damage
  • NC3Rs: Terminal anesthesia only
  • Alternatives: Tarsal vein, tail vein, facial vein

Ethical Status

  • Banned or restricted in several countries
  • Scientific justification required by IACUCs
  • Refined methods preferred under 3Rs principle
  • Training and proficiency critical to reduce harm
