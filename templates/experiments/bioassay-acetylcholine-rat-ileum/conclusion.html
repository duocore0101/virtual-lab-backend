Result & Conclusion

Result

The concentration of the unknown/test Acetylcholine (ACh) solution in the given sample was found to be:

97.5 – 100 μg/mL

(Close to standard concentration, indicating good bracketing and parallelism.)

Potency comparison:

  • The unknown sample is approximately equipotent to the standard solution.
  • The test responses were well bracketed between S1 and S2 doses.
  • Parallel dose-response relationship was observed.

In many practical settings, the calculated concentration may vary between 80–120 μg/mL depending on tissue response and experimental conditions.

Conclusion

The experiment confirms the validity of the Four-Point Bioassay Method for determining the potency of Acetylcholine using isolated rat ileum.

The assay demonstrated:

  • Proper dose-dependent increase in smooth muscle contraction.
  • Good reproducibility between cycles.
  • Stable tissue preparation throughout the experiment.

Therefore, the bioassay method is reliable and suitable for estimation of unknown ACh concentration in pharmacology practicals.
