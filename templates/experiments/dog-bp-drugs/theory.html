Theory / Principle

Arterial Blood Pressure

Arterial blood pressure is the lateral pressure exerted by blood on the walls of arteries. It is determined by the relationship:

Blood Pressure =
Cardiac Output × Total Peripheral Resistance

Autonomic Regulation of Cardiovascular System

The cardiovascular system is primarily regulated by the autonomic nervous system, which maintains blood pressure and heart rate according to physiological demands.

Sympathetic Stimulation

Sympathetic neurotransmitters such as adrenaline and noradrenaline increase cardiac output by stimulating β1 receptors and increase peripheral resistance by activating α receptors, resulting in a rise in blood pressure.

Parasympathetic Stimulation

Parasympathetic stimulation mediated by acetylcholine acting on muscarinic receptors decreases heart rate and cardiac output, leading to hypotension and bradycardia.

Baroreceptor Reflex

A sudden rise in blood pressure activates baroreceptors in the carotid sinus and aortic arch, resulting in reflex bradycardia through vagal stimulation. This experiment demonstrates chronotropic and inotropic effects along with receptor-specific drug actions.
