Theory/Principle

Pylorus Ligation (Shay Rat) Model

The pylorus ligation model was introduced by Shay et al. (1945) to study gastric ulcer formation. In this method the pyloric end of the stomach is ligated, preventing gastric emptying.

Due to this obstruction, gastric secretions accumulate in the stomach leading to excessive secretion of hydrochloric acid and pepsin. The continuous accumulation of gastric juice causes auto-digestion of the gastric mucosa.

This results in the formation of acute peptic ulcers, especially in the forestomach or rumen region of rats within 4–19 hours.

Drugs that decrease gastric acid secretion or improve mucosal defense mechanisms reduce ulcer formation in this model.

Evaluation of Anti-ulcer Drugs

The pylorus ligation model is widely used for evaluating the anti-secretory activity of drugs such as H₂-receptor blockers and proton pump inhibitors (PPIs).

Anti-ulcer drugs reduce gastric acidity and increase mucosal protection, thereby preventing ulcer formation.

The following parameters are commonly evaluated:

  • Volume of gastric juice
  • Free acidity
  • Total acidity
  • pH of gastric contents
  • Ulcer index (severity of lesions)

A decrease in free and total acidity, an increase in gastric pH, and a reduction in ulcer index indicate significant anti-ulcer activity.

NSAID-Induced Ulcer Model

Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as indomethacin and aspirin can induce gastric ulcers in experimental animals.

NSAIDs inhibit the enzyme cyclooxygenase-1 (COX-1), which reduces the synthesis of protective prostaglandins (PGE₂).

Prostaglandins normally protect the gastric mucosa by increasing mucus and bicarbonate secretion, maintaining mucosal blood flow and regulating acid secretion.

When prostaglandin production decreases, the gastric mucosa becomes vulnerable to damage from gastric acid, leading to erosions and ulcers in the stomach.

Pretreatment with anti-ulcer drugs such as omeprazole, ranitidine or cytoprotective agents can significantly reduce lesion formation in this model.
