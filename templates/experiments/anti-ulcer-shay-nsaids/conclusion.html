Result & Conclusion

Result

The experiment demonstrated the anti-ulcer activity of the test drug using the pylorus-ligated (Shay rat) model and NSAID-induced ulcer model in rats.

It was observed that:

  • The test drug significantly reduced gastric juice volume compared to the ulcer control group.
  • There was a significant decrease in free acidity and total acidity and a corresponding increase in gastric pH.
  • The ulcer index was markedly reduced in animals treated with the test drug.
  • In the NSAID-induced ulcer model, the drug significantly reduced ulcer severity and gastric lesions.
  • These effects were comparable to the standard anti-ulcer drug such as ranitidine or omeprazole.

Conclusion

  • The pylorus ligation model is useful for evaluating anti-secretory drugs that reduce gastric acid output.
  • NSAID-induced ulcer models help in studying drugs that provide gastroprotective and cytoprotective effects.
  • Reduction in gastric acidity, ulcer index and lesion severity indicates significant anti-ulcer activity.
  • Experimental animal models are valuable tools for evaluating pharmacological activity of anti-ulcer drugs, though they may not fully replicate human gastric diseases.
  • Therefore these models help in the screening and development of new anti-ulcer agents.
