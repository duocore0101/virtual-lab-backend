Principle

Guinea pig ileum contracts in response to histamine via activation of H1 receptors present on smooth muscle.

The Matching Method (also called bracketing or trial-and-error method) is used when the test sample volume is limited and precise comparison is required.

Responses (contractions) to increasing doses of standard histamine are first recorded.

The test (unknown) dose is then added repeatedly until its contraction height approximately matches one of the standard responses.

The dose of standard that produces the matching response indicates the potency of the test sample.

Receptor: H1 receptor
Tissue: Isolated guinea pig ileum
Response: Smooth muscle contraction
Method: Matching (Bracketing) Method
