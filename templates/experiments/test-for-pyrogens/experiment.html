Test for Pyrogens

✖

Procedure

  1. You are already aware that injectable preparations must be free from pyrogen contamination. The test for pyrogen is designed to measure the rise in temperature in rabbit due to contaminated injectables.
  2. An apparatus telethermometer with six probes is used for the test. It consist of a mains on/off switch, digital counter, rotating knob, six probes and sockets for measurement of temperature. Calibration of telethermometer is a prerequisite.
  3. To check the test sample for pyrogen, always select three healthy rabbits of either sex.
  4. These animals must be previously conditioned and housed according to IP. Place the animals individually in rabbit holders.
  5. Now insert the temperature sensing probe in to the rectum up to a depth of about 5cm.
  6. Record the normal body temperature of each animal before the injection of test sample every 30 minutes for a span of at least ninety minutes. Do not use any rabbit showing body temperature higher than 39.8 degree and lower than 38 degree centigrade.
  7. Now inject the test sample in to the ear vein of each rabbit. Here, water for injection is used. The volume of injection is not less than 0.5ml/kg.
  8. Record the temperature of each rabbit at every half hour for three hours duration after the injection. You shall observe the rise in body temperature of all 3 individual rabbits is less than 0.6 degrees centigrade. This is indicative of the fact that the test sample is free from pyrogen.
