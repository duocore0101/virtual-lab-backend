Theory Behind The Experiment

Pyrogens are high molecular weight lipopolysaccharides. If introduced into the human body, they cause transient fever. The constituents of the outer membrane of the bacterial cell wall have pyrogenic activity; such constituents are known as endotoxins.

Injectable preparations must be free from pyrogens, so efforts are made to prevent pyrogen contamination during manufacture. Methods are also available to test the end product, whether they are free from pyrogen or not.

The pyrogen test is designed to measure the rise in temperature of three rabbits following intravenous injection of the test sample. The dose of test sample should not exceed 10 ml/kg, given IV within a period of 10 minutes. The sample is injected into the marginal ear vein of rabbits at 37°C (± 2°C). Temperature is recorded at regular intervals of 30 minutes after the injection.

To record temperature of test animals, an accurate temperature sensing device such as a clinical thermometer or telethermometer is preferred. Such device should be calibrated to assure accuracy of ±0.1°C.

In such case, the temperature sensing probe is inserted into the rectum of the test rabbit up to a depth of about 5 cm.
