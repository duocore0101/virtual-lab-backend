Result & Conclusion

Observations : Interpretation of Result

If the sum of the responses of the group of three rabbits does not exceed 1.4°C and if the response of any individual rabbit is less than 0.6°C, the preparation being examined passes the test.

If the responses of any rabbit is 0.6°C or more and if the sum of responses of three rabbits exceeds 1.4°C, continue test by using another five rabbits.

If not more than three of eight rabbits show individual responses of 0.6°C or more and if sum of responses of group eight rabbits does not exceed 3.7°C, the preparation complies with the test of pyrogen.

Conclusion

  • Telethermometer with six probes is used as an apparatus/device in recording fever response.
  • Prerequisites for the test must be strictly adhered.
  • A preparation is said to be having passed the test for pyrogen only if:
    • The sum of the responses of a group of three rabbits does not exceed 1.4°C.
    • The response of any individual rabbit should be less than 0.6°C.
  • Failure to obtain the above result test must be repeated employing five other rabbits, in all eight rabbits are employed in the test.
  • Under this condition not more than three of the eight rabbits should show individual responses of 0.6°C or more and sum of responses of the group of eight rabbits does not exceed 3.7°C.
  • It is only then a preparation is said to be finally passed the test for pyrogen.
