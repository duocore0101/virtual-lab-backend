Comparative Effect on Pupil Diameter

Conclusion

The present experiment demonstrates the effects of autonomic drugs on the rabbit eye using a virtual simulation model.

Atropine produces mydriasis by blocking muscarinic receptors, leading to dilation of the pupil and loss of light reflex.

Pilocarpine produces miosis by stimulating muscarinic receptors, resulting in constriction of the pupil with enhanced reflex activity.

The experiment confirms opposite pharmacological effects of parasympathetic blockers and stimulants on the eye.
