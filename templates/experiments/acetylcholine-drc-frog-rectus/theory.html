Theory / Principle

Principle of the Experiment

The rectus abdominis muscle of the frog is a skeletal muscle that possesses nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs). Acetylcholine acts as a full agonist at these receptors, producing a slow, sustained contracture (tonic contraction) in a dose-dependent graded manner.

Receptor Occupancy Theory

According to the occupancy theory and the law of mass action, increasing concentrations of the agonist lead to greater receptor occupancy and thus greater response until receptor saturation occurs (ceiling effect).

Log Dose–Response Relationship

When responses are plotted against log dose (or log concentration), a characteristic sigmoid (S-shaped) curve is obtained, representing graded pharmacological response.

Phases of Sigmoid Curve

  • Initial Flat Portion → Low sensitivity (threshold doses)
  • Middle Steep Linear Portion → High discrimination to dose changes
  • Upper Plateau → Maximum efficacy (all receptors occupied)

Pharmacological Significance

This experiment demonstrates graded pharmacological response, helps determine potency (pD₂), EC₅₀, and efficacy of acetylcholine, and serves as a model for studying receptor-mediated drug actions.
