Result & Conclusion

Result

The dose response curve of acetylcholine on frog rectus abdominis muscle was found to be sigmoid-shaped when plotted on a log dose vs. response graph, confirming a receptor-mediated graded response.

Acetylcholine acts as a full agonist at nicotinic receptors, producing sustained contracture with:

  • Threshold dose ≈ 0.1–0.2 μg
  • Ceiling dose ≈ 6.4–12.8 μg (in 10 mL bath)
  • EC₅₀ ≈ 0.2–0.8 μg (≈ 0.1–0.5 μM)

Conclusion

The experiment successfully demonstrates the quantitative relationship between agonist concentration and tissue response. The graded dose-response behavior confirms the principles of receptor occupancy theory and the law of mass action.

This model is widely used in pharmacology for studying cholinergic drugs, determining potency (pD₂), and performing bioassay experiments.
