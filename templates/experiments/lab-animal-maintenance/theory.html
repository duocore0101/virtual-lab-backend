To study the maintenance of laboratory animals according to CPCSEA guidelines

Principle

The Committee for the Purpose of Control and Supervision of Experiments on Animals (CPCSEA), under the Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying (Government of India), provides detailed guidelines for humane care, housing, and maintenance of laboratory animals. These guidelines aim to ensure Good Laboratory Practices (GLP), animal welfare, quality maintenance, and safety during biomedical and behavioral research.

Procurement and Arrival

  • Animals must be procured only from CPCSEA-registered breeders.
  • Health certificates and transport conditions must be verified.
  • Animals should be transferred immediately to quarantine.
  • Cages must be labeled with species, strain, source, date, and number.
  • Acclimatization period of at least 7 days for rodents is required.

Quarantine and Health Monitoring

  • Animals are housed in a separate quarantine room.
  • Daily observation for signs of illness is mandatory.
  • Health screening includes fecal and microbiological tests.
  • Prophylactic treatment as advised by veterinarian.
  • Animals are released only after veterinary clearance

Housing Setup

Appropriate cages must be used: polycarbonate or polypropylene cages for rodents and stainless steel cages for larger animals. Clean, dust-free bedding and environmental enrichment such as nesting material or tunnels should be provided.g.

Environmental Control

  • Temperature: 22–26°C (rodents), 18–22°C (rabbits)
  • Humidity: 40–70%
  • Ventilation: 10–15 air changes/hour
  • Lighting: 12:12 light–dark cycle
  • Noise should be minimized (<85 dB)

Feeding and Watering

  • Nutritionally balanced pellet diet should be provided ad libitum.
  • Feed hoppers must be checked daily.
  • Clean potable water should be supplied daily.
  • Food or water restriction only with protocol approval.

Daily Maintenance

  • Personnel must wear PPE before entering animal rooms.
  • Animals are observed daily for behavior and health.
  • Observations must be recorded in daily logs.
  • Soiled bedding is spot-cleaned and waste removed.
  • Any abnormality must be reported to the veterinarian.

Veterinary Care & Records

  • Periodic cleaning and sanitization of cages is essential.
  • Veterinary care includes treatment and humane euthanasia.
  • Maintain records of inventory, health, environment, and protocols.
  • Annual reports must be submitted to CPCSEA.
