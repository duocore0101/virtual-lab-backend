Result & Conclusion

Result

The bioassay of oxytocin on isolated rat uterine horn produced a graded dose-dependent contraction. The dose-response curve plotted between log dose and response height was sigmoid in shape, confirming receptor-mediated smooth muscle contraction.

From the standard curve:

  • Threshold dose ≈ 0.1 mL
  • Maximum response observed at 0.8 mL
  • Linear working range ≈ 20–80% response

Conclusion

The bioassay of oxytocin was successfully performed on the isolated rat uterine horn using the interpolation method. A standard dose-response curve was plotted, and the concentration of the unknown sample was determined by interpolating its response on the linear portion of the curve.

The experiment demonstrates that oxytocin produces dose-dependent uterine contractions and that the interpolation method provides an accurate estimation of its biological potency.
