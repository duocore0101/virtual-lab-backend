Result & Conclusion

Result

The basal locomotor activity of mice was recorded using a digital actophotometer prior to drug administration. Following intraperitoneal administration of the test drugs, a significant change in locomotor activity was observed when compared with pre-drug values.

Chlorpromazine (CNS depressant) produced a marked reduction in locomotor activity, whereas Caffeine (CNS stimulant) caused a significant increase in locomotor counts.

Conclusion

The present experiment demonstrates that drugs affecting the central nervous system significantly alter spontaneous locomotor activity in mice.

Chlorpromazine exhibited a 50–70% decrease in locomotor activity, confirming its CNS depressant property. In contrast, caffeine produced a 30–150% increase in activity, indicating its CNS stimulant action.
