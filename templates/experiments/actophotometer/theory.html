Theory/Principle

Principle of Actophotometer

The actophotometer measures spontaneous locomotor activity of laboratory animals. The activity is directly proportional to the level of central nervous system (CNS) excitation.

Working Mechanism

The apparatus consists of an activity cage with photocell beams. Each movement of the animal interrupts the light beam, and these interruptions are recorded as digital counts.

Effect of CNS Depressants

CNS depressant drugs such as chlorpromazine and diazepam reduce neuronal excitability, leading to decreased locomotor activity and lower activity counts.

Effect of CNS Stimulants

CNS stimulant drugs such as caffeine and amphetamine increase neuronal firing, resulting in enhanced locomotor activity and higher activity counts.

Interpretation

A decrease in locomotor counts indicates CNS depressant activity, whereas an increase in counts indicates CNS stimulant activity. Thus, the actophotometer is a reliable screening method.
