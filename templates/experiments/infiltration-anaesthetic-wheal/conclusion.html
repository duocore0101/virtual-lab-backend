Result & Conclusion

Result

The test drugs were evaluated for their ability to produce infiltration anaesthesia using the guinea pig wheal method.

  • Lignocaine HCl 1%: Produced rapid onset of infiltration anaesthesia with prolonged duration, indicated by absence of response to pin prick in the wheal area.
  • Procaine HCl 1%: Produced infiltration anaesthesia with slightly delayed onset and shorter duration compared to lignocaine.
  • Normal Saline: Did not produce anaesthesia and showed normal pain response throughout the observation period.

Loss of skin pain sensation in the wheal area confirms effective blockade of cutaneous nerve endings.

Conclusion

The test drugs produce infiltration anaesthesia by blocking voltage-gated sodium channels in peripheral sensory nerve fibers, thereby preventing conduction of pain impulses.

The absence of response to pin prick in the wheal region confirms successful infiltration anaesthetic activity. The study demonstrates the effectiveness of local anaesthetics in producing reversible loss of cutaneous sensation.

Precautions

  • Shave the dorsal area properly before injection.
  • Ensure intradermal injection to form a proper wheal.
  • Use minimal volume and correct concentration of drug.
  • Observe animal carefully during pin prick testing.
  • Follow CPCSEA / IAEC ethical guidelines strictly.
