Theory/Principle

Introduction

Acute eye irritation is the production of reversible changes in the eye (cornea, iris, or conjunctiva) following application of a test substance. Acute eye corrosion (serious eye damage) refers to irreversible tissue damage or effects that are not fully reversible within 21 days.

Principle of OECD 405 Test

Following pretreatment with systemic analgesic and topical anaesthetic, a single dose of the test substance is instilled into the conjunctival sac of one eye of the rabbit (untreated eye serves as control). Ocular reactions are scored using the Draize scoring system at specified intervals. Observations are continued for up to 21 days to determine reversibility or irreversibility of lesions. Humane endpoints are strictly applied.
