Theory/Principle

Principle

A focused radiant heat stimulus is applied to the tail, and the latency to tail withdrawal (tail flick) is measured.

This is primarily a spinal reflex and is sensitive to centrally acting analgesics (especially opioids) which increase pain threshold.

Animal: Albino rats/mice
Heat Source: Radiant heat
Cut-off Time: 10 seconds
Reaction: Tail withdrawal
