Result & Conclusion

Result

The experiment demonstrates the protective effect of Diphenhydramine (an antihistamine drug) against histamine-induced bronchospasm in experimental animals.

Administration of histamine aerosol produced bronchospasm, difficulty in breathing and abnormal behaviour in the control animal.

After administration of Diphenhydramine HCl, the animal showed normal behaviour and normal breathing, indicating antagonism of histamine action.

Conclusion

  • Administration of histamine is associated with anaphylactic reactions and bronchospasm and is not completely recovered by diphenhydramine alone.
  • Asthmatic airway narrowing occurs mainly due to inflammation of respiratory passages.
  • Allergens are responsible for the release of histamine which triggers allergic reactions.
  • Antagonism of constrictor action of histamine on respiratory smooth muscles can be studied by producing asthma in laboratory animals.
  • Speedy recovery from shock and protection to animals can be achieved by administration of adrenaline along with diphenhydramine.
