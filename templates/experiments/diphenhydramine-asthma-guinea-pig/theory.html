Theory Behind The Experiment

A person over reactive to a substance tolerated by most other is said to be hypersensitive i.e. allergic. Substances which induce allergic reactions are allergens.

Common allergens are milk, antibiotics like penicillin, vaccines like typhoid vaccine, snake venom, pollens, dust, moulds etc.

The most common reaction is anaphylactic reaction and occurs within few minutes of exposure to allergen.

In this reaction, IgE-antibody sensitized mast cell and basophils release histamine, prostaglandins and kinins.

These mediators cause vasodilatation, increased capillary permeability, increased smooth muscle contraction of lungs and increased mucus secretion.

As a result there is difficulty in breathing, runny nose and wheezing, along with shock due to vasodilatation.

Such condition can be treated with adrenaline injection and some antihistamines.

Although the guinea pig has been favored for screening anti-histamine agents because of its sensitivity to histamine, the rabbit may serve when a second species is desired.

With the help of histamine aerosol chamber one can test effect of histamine and antihistamines like diphenhydramine on rabbit.

Antihistamine antagonizes the effect of histamine and improvement in signs and symptoms of asthma can be observed.
