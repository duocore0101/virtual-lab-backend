Result & Conclusion

Result

The mast cell degranulation method was used to evaluate the anti-allergic potential of the test drug by observing intact and degranulated mast cells.

The standard drug showed maximum mast cell stabilization compared to the disease control group.

The test drug showed a dose-dependent increase in mast cell protection.

The high dose of the test drug showed significant anti-allergic activity.

Conclusion

Mast cell stabilization is an important mechanism in preventing allergic reactions by inhibiting the release of inflammatory mediators.

The test drug demonstrated significant mast cell stabilizing (anti-degranulation) activity, confirming its potential as an effective anti-allergic agent.

The dose-dependent response further supports the pharmacological efficacy of the drug in controlling allergic conditions.
