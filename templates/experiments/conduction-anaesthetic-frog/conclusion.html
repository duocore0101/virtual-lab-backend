Result & Conclusion

Result

The test drug produced loss of withdrawal reflex when applied around the sciatic nerve trunk of frog, indicating successful conduction block.

Observations showed:

  • Onset of action: 2–3 minutes
  • Duration of anaesthesia: 28–45 minutes
  • Reflex response: Absent during effective period
  • Control limb: Normal reflex maintained

Conclusion

The experiment demonstrates that the test drug produces conduction anaesthesia by blocking impulse transmission along peripheral nerve fibers.

The loss of reflex withdrawal confirms local anaesthetic action via reversible inhibition of voltage-gated Na⁺ channels in the sciatic nerve trunk.

Thus, the test drug produces conduction block (loss of reflex via nerve trunk blockade), confirming local anaesthetic action on peripheral nerves.
