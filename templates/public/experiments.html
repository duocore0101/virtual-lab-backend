  2. Commonly used instruments in experimental pharmacology.
  3. Study of common laboratory animals in experimental pharmacology.
  4. Introduction to in-vitro pharmacology and physiological salt solutions.
  5. Study of dose calculation and stock solution preparation in pharmacological experiments.

  1. Maintenance of laboratory animals as per CPCSEA guidelines (handling & restraining).
  2. Common laboratory techniques: Blood withdrawal; Serum and plasma separation; Anaesthetics and euthanasia for animal studies.
  3. Study of different routes of drug administration in small animals (rats/mice).

  1. Effect of drugs on rabbit eye (pupil size / intraocular pressure).
  2. Effect of drugs on frog’s ciliary motility in frog oesophagus.
  3. Effect of agonists and antagonists on guinea pig ileum.
  4. Effect of saline purgative on frog intestine.

  6. Effect of drugs on gastrointestinal motility in rat/mice.

  1. Effect of skeletal muscle relaxants using Rota-rod apparatus.
  2. Effect of drugs on locomotor activity using actophotometer.
  3. Study of stereotype & anti-catatonic activity of drugs on rat/mice.
  4. Anticonvulsant effect of drugs by MES and PTZ method.
  5. Study of anxiolytic activity of drugs using elevated plus maze method.
  6. Taming and hypnosis potentiating effect of chlorpromazine in mice/rat.

  1. Study of isolation and perfusion of frog heart.

  4. Study of effect of acetylcholine and adrenaline on isolated frog heart.

  6. Study of diuretic activity of drugs using rats/mice.

  2. Evaluation of analgesic activity by tail flick method.

  1. Study of insulin-induced hypoglycaemic effect in rabbit.

  1. Study of anti-ulcer activity using pylorus-ligated (Shay) rat model and NSAIDs-induced ulcer model.

  1. Study of local anaesthetic by different methods.
  2. Evaluation of anti-allergic activity by mast cell stabilization assay.
  3. Effect of diphenhydramine in experimentally produced asthma in guinea pig.

  1. Introduction to bioassay & assembly setup.
  2. Dose-response curve (DRC) of acetylcholine using frog rectus abdominis muscle.

  6. Bioassay of serotonin (5-HT) using rat fundus strip by three-point method.
  7. Bioassay of acetylcholine using rat ileum/colon by four-point method.
  8. Determination of PA₂ value of prazosin using rat anococcygenus muscle (Schild’s plot method).
  9. Determination of PD₂ value using guinea pig ileum.

  1. Determination of acute oral toxicity (LD₅₀) of a drug from given data.
  2. Determination of acute skin irritation/corrosion of a test substance.
  3. Determination of acute eye irritation/corrosion of a test substance.

L. Biochemical, Pharmacokinetics & Biostatistics

  1. Estimation of serum biochemical parameters using semi-autoanalyser.
  2. Calculation of pharmacokinetic parameters from given data.
  3. Biostatistical methods in experimental pharmacology: Student’s t-test; ANOVA; Chi-square test; Wilcoxon signed-rank test.
